Noise & Drivability Diagnosis in Smyrna, TN

That clunk when you hit a bump. The squeal when you brake. The vibration at highway speed. The way your car drifts to one side. These aren't just annoyances — they're your vehicle telling you something is wrong. Ignoring them often means a worse problem (and a bigger repair bill) down the road.

At CarTech Automotive, we take noise and drivability complaints seriously. We do a systematic inspection — not just a quick look — to find the actual source. That means a thorough test drive, a lift inspection of the suspension, steering, brakes, and drivetrain, and careful attention to your description of when and how the issue happens.

We service all makes and models, domestic and import. Whether it's a worn ball joint, a bad wheel bearing, warped rotors, a failing CV axle, or something harder to pin down, we'll find it and give you a straight answer on what it needs.

Why Choose CarTech for Noise & Drivability

  • Systematic diagnosis, not guesswork — we test drive, inspect, and listen. We don't throw parts at it and hope for the best.

Noise & Drivability — Frequently Asked Questions

Clunking noises are commonly caused by worn ball joints, struts, sway bar end links, CV axles, or loose brake hardware. The location of the noise and when it happens — turning, braking, going over bumps — helps narrow it down. Bring it in and we'll do a thorough inspection to find the exact source.

Brake-related vibration is most commonly caused by warped brake rotors. When rotors develop thickness variation from heat cycling, you'll feel a pulsating or shuddering sensation through the pedal and steering wheel. In some cases it's a stuck caliper causing uneven braking. Either way, it's a safety issue that should be addressed promptly.

It can be. Pulling is often caused by uneven tire pressure, a tire with belt separation, an alignment issue, or uneven brake wear from a sticking caliper. Some of these are safety concerns — if the pull is significant or came on suddenly, get it checked before driving further.

Intermittent noises are some of the trickiest to diagnose. We start with a thorough test drive to try to reproduce it, then a lift inspection of the suspension, steering, brakes, and drivetrain. Your description of when it happens, at what speed, and under what conditions is a major part of the diagnostic process — we take that information seriously.

It depends on the source. A rattling heat shield is annoying but not dangerous. A clunking ball joint or vibration under braking is a different story. When in doubt, call us at 615-220-6114 — describe what you're hearing and when, and we'll give you an honest read on whether it's urgent.
